Bentota Turtle Hatchery, situated on the southwestern coast of Sri Lanka, is a notable conservation center dedicated to the preservation of sea turtles. Nestled amidst pristine coastal surroundings, the hatchery offers a unique opportunity for visitors to learn about the importance of turtle conservation and actively participate in efforts to protect these magnificent creatures.

At Bentota Turtle Hatchery, visitors can witness the incredible process of sea turtles coming ashore to lay their eggs. Knowledgeable staff members guide guests through the hatchery, providing valuable information about the various turtle species found in the region, including the Olive Ridley, Green, Hawksbill, and Loggerhead turtles. Guests can observe the careful collection and relocation of eggs to ensure their safety and maximize the chances of successful hatching.

One of the most fascinating experiences at Bentota Turtle Hatchery is the opportunity to release baby turtles into the ocean. The hatchlings are nurtured in specially designed tanks until they are strong enough to make their journey back to the sea. Visitors can actively participate in this crucial moment, releasing the tiny turtles onto the sandy beach and witnessing their determined crawl towards the water. This hands-on engagement fosters a sense of connection and responsibility towards the well-being of sea turtles.
